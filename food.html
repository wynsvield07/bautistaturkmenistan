Turkmen Cuisine

Hearty, flavorful dishes shaped by nomadic traditions

Featured Dish

Plov

The national dish of Central Asia — a rich rice pilaf cooked with lamb, carrots, and onions in a large cast-iron kazan. Fragrant and deeply satisfying.

Ishlekli (Meat Pie)

A traditional shepherd’s dish made with minced meat and onions between two layers of dough.

Dograma

A savory dish prepared by tearing fresh bread and mixing it with boiled mutton and onions.

Chorek

A traditional, crusty flatbread baked in a tamdyr, holding significant cultural value.

Shorpa

A rich, hearty soup filled with lamb, potatoes, and tomatoes.

Food & Hospitality

In Turkmen culture, offering food is an act of deep respect. Guests are always served tea (çay) first, followed by bread. Traditional meals are eaten communally around a dastarkhan — a decorative tablecloth spread on the ground.
