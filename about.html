About Turkmenistan

Discover the land, language, and culture

Geography

Turkmenistan is a Central Asian country bordered by Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Afghanistan, Iran, and the Caspian Sea. About 80% of its territory is covered by the vast Karakum Desert.

Despite its arid landscape, Turkmenistan holds the world's fourth largest natural gas reserves. The Amu Darya river and the Karakum Canal provide vital water resources.

Language

Turkmen is the official state language, belonging to the Oghuz branch of the Turkic language family, closely related to Turkish and Azerbaijani.

Russian remains widely used in business and urban settings. The alphabet transitioned from Cyrillic to Latin-based script in 1993 after independence.

A common Turkmen phrase:

"Salam!" — Hello!

Culture

Carpets

Turkmen handmade carpets are world-renowned, featuring intricate geometric patterns. Each tribe has its own unique designs.

Hospitality

Guest culture is central to Turkmen life. Visitors are always welcomed with tea, bread, and generous meals.

Nomadic Roots

Much of Turkmen culture traces back to nomadic traditions — from yurt architecture to oral poetry and horsemanship.
