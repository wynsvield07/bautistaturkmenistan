Darvaza Gas Crater

A 70-meter wide burning crater in the Karakum Desert that has been flaming since 1971. Best experienced at night when the orange glow contrasts against the dark sky.

Ashgabat

The capital city holds the world record for the highest concentration of white marble buildings. Key sights include the Independence Monument and the Arch of Neutrality.

Ancient Merv

A UNESCO World Heritage site and once one of the largest cities in the world along the Silk Road. Highlights include the Sultan Sanjar Mausoleum and Great Kyz Kala.

Yangykala Canyon

Known for its "otherworldly" landscape of pink, red, and yellow rock formations, often described as one of the most beautiful natural spots in the country.

Old Nisa

Located just 15km from Ashgabat, these are the ruins of the ancient capital of the Parthian Empire. A UNESCO site highly recommended to visit with a guide.
